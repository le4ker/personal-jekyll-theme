About

Iosif, or __Joseph__, as he likes being called, is a __Machine Learning__ professional specialised in __GIS technologies__. But, first and foremost, he is an **Engineer** who enjoys planning and building projects from the ground up. During his studying at the School of Spatial Planning and Development Engineering of the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, he learned how to deeply analyze and evaluate the living space. He learned how the elaborate search for weaknesses can prove most valuable for the design of developmental policies. Github's Octocat was designed by Simon Oxley, alongside the white bird Twitter used (before they received a proper logo) as part of a usual routine of cranking out images for iStock. GitHub saw it, and wanted it, presumably under the notion that it can represent how complex code combines to create peculiar things, much like the octopuss... except the CEO of GitHub called it an octocat, and it has been the octocat since then. Octocat is Github's logo and trademark and here is used to illustrate a fictional character (John Smith) that used the *{ Personal }* Jekyll Theme to build his website.
